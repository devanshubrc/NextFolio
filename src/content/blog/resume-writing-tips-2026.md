# Top 5 Resume Writing Guidelines for Tech Professionals

A technical resume needs to be more than a simple list of daily duties. It must showcase your engineering ownership and quantify the impact of your contributions. 

Follow these 5 expert guidelines to transform your resume into a highly competitive marketing document.

## 1. Quantify Your Achievements
Hiring managers love numbers because they show the scale and result of your work. Avoid writing passive tasks like "Responsible for writing backend scripts." Instead, write:
> "Designed and optimized backend database triggers, reducing overall query latency by **35%** for **12k daily active users**."

## 2. Start Every Bullet with an Action Verb
Start your bullet points with active verbs that convey leadership and technical execution (e.g. Developed, Spearheaded, Refactored, Architected). Remove weak, passive qualifiers like "Assisted with," "Worked on," or "Help with."

## 3. Structure with the STAR Method
For major projects, explain the context clearly:
*   **Situation:** What was the business need or bottleneck?
*   **Task:** What was your assigned objective?
*   **Action:** What specific tools, frameworks, and architecture did you select and execute?
*   **Result:** What was the outcome of your actions (speed improvements, code reduction)?

## 4. Curate Your Technical Skills
Organize your skills section into readable categories:
*   **Languages:** TypeScript, JavaScript, Go, SQL
*   **Frameworks:** React, Next.js, Express, Tailwind
*   **Tools:** Git, Docker, Kubernetes, AWS

This keeps the section clean and matches the keywords search queries recruiters run inside their ATS systems.
